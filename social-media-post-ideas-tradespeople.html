30 Social Media Post Ideas for Tradespeople (With Examples, 2026)

Steal these. Adapt the wording to your trade and your job, then post. Examples in orange are written the way a real tradesperson talks.

"I do not know what to post" is the number one reason trade social accounts go quiet. So here are 30 ideas, each with a real-sounding example you can adapt in seconds. Pick one, change the details to match your job, and you have a post.

Finished work (the bread and butter)

  1. The job you just finished.Wrapped up a full rewire in Thatcham today. Old fuse board gone, new consumer unit in, everything tested and certified. Lights stay on, that is the idea.
  2. A before-and-after.Swipe to see this kitchen go from tired and dark to bright and modern. Two weeks of graft, one very happy family.
  3. A tricky problem you solved.Customer had a leak nobody could find for months. Traced it to a hairline crack behind the bath. Sorted in an afternoon.
  4. A close-up detail you are proud of.It is the little things. Neat cable runs, every screw lined up. If you cut corners where nobody looks, you cut them everywhere.
  5. A bigger project, in stages.Day three of this extension. Footings done, walls going up. Will keep you posted as it takes shape.

Helpful tips (the kind that pull in enquiries)

  1. How to spot a problem early.Brown patch on your ceiling? Do not paint over it. It is almost always a leak above. Catch it now and it is cheap. Leave it and it is not.
  2. A common mistake homeowners make.Please do not put wet wipes down the loo, even the ones that say flushable. They are the number one cause of the blockages we get called to.
  3. How long a job actually takes."How long for a bathroom?" Usually 7 to 10 working days done properly. Anyone promising two is cutting something you will regret.
  4. A seasonal reminder.Cold snap coming. Bleed your radiators if the tops are cold and the bottoms warm. Two minutes, much cosier house.
  5. What to check before you buy a house.Viewing a property? Look at the consumer unit and the boiler age. Those two tell you a lot about what you are walking into.

Behind the scenes (the human stuff people love)

  1. The van loaded up at the start of the day.
  2. The team on site, sleeves rolled up.
  3. The tool you could not work without.
  4. An early start with a view, or a brew on a cold morning.
  5. A quick clip of the work in progress.

Trust and proof (turn happy customers into new ones)

  1. A review, shared with a thank you.This made our week. Thank you Sarah. Jobs like yours are exactly why we love what we do.
  2. A repeat customer.Third time back for this family now. There is no better compliment in this trade than being asked again.
  3. Your certifications or memberships, explained simply.
  4. The standard of workmanship you stand behind.
  5. How you leave a site (clean and tidy).We leave it cleaner than we found it. Every time. You should not be able to tell we were here, except the work is better.

Local and practical (be findable in your area)

  1. The areas you cover.Covering Newbury, Thatcham, Hungerford and the villages in between. If you are local and need a hand, give us a shout.
  2. A slot you have open.Had a job move, so we have an open week starting Monday. If you have been putting something off, now is a good time.
  3. A local job people will recognise.
  4. Who you are and how you started.
  5. What makes you different from the next firm.

Quick and easy fillers (for a slow week)

  1. A myth in your trade, busted.
  2. A this-or-that question to your followers.
  3. A throwback to a job from last year.
  4. A simple reminder of how to get a quote from you.
  5. A genuine thank you to your customers for the year so far.
