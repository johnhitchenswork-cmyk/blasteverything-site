Cross-Posting App vs Social Media Scheduler vs Doing It Yourself: What Fits a UK Trade? (2026)

An honest look at the three ways tradespeople handle social media, and which one actually suits a busy trade business.

If you run a trade business and you have decided to take social media seriously, you have three real options. Post everything manually, use a general social media scheduler built for marketers, or use a cross-posting app built to turn one message into posts everywhere. They sound similar. In day-to-day use they are very different. Here is the honest comparison.

Quick verdict

  • Best for a busy tradesperson: a one-message cross-posting app, because it removes the per-platform work that kills consistency.
  • Best for a dedicated marketing person: a full scheduler with calendars and analytics, if someone has time to run it.
  • Best for nobody, long term: doing it all manually, because it is the first thing to get dropped when work gets busy.

Option 1: Doing it yourself manually

This is where most trades start. You finish a job, take a photo, and post it to Facebook. Maybe Instagram too if you have the energy. The work is fine. The problem is repetition and willpower.

To be present everywhere your customers are, you would write the post, then rewrite it for the next platform, then the next, opening and closing apps as you go. It is the kind of fiddly admin that always loses to a paying job. So the honest outcome is that manual posting starts strong and goes quiet within a month or two.

Best for: testing the water on a single platform before you commit. Not a long-term answer if you want to be visible across the board.

Option 2: A general social media scheduler

Schedulers and social media management tools are powerful. They offer content calendars, scheduling weeks ahead, team approvals and analytics dashboards. For a business with someone whose actual job is marketing, they are excellent.

The catch for a trade is that all that power assumes someone has time to sit and run it. Calendars, queues and dashboards are overhead when you are on the tools all day. You are paying for, and learning, features built for a marketing department you do not have.

Best for: larger firms with a dedicated marketing person, or trades who genuinely enjoy planning content in advance.

The part that decides it: will you actually keep doing it?

Every approach can technically post to social media. The real question is which one you will still be using in three months. Manual posting loses to a busy week. A heavy scheduler loses to the time it takes to run. A one-message app survives because posting takes less time than making a brew.

Consistency is what wins local work, not clever tools you abandon. So the right choice is the one you will keep using when the diary is full, which for most trades is the simplest one.

Choose a cross-posting app if you

  • Are on the tools all day with no time for admin
  • Want to be on several platforms, not just one
  • Want posting to take under a minute
  • Do not want to learn marketing software

Choose a full scheduler if you

  • Have a dedicated marketing person
  • Plan content weeks in advance
  • Need detailed analytics and approvals
  • Run a larger team with many seats
