For builders, plumbers, electricians, roofers and local service firms who are too busy on the tools to fiddle with apps.

1. Post to all your channels from one message

This is the single biggest time-saver, which is why it is first. The slow part of social media has never been writing one post. It is rewriting it for Facebook, then Instagram, then LinkedIn, then Google, then jumping between apps to publish each one. A cross-posting tool removes that entirely. You write one update about a job, and it goes out, formatted for each platform, in one go.

Blast Everything is built for this and aimed squarely at UK trades. One message becomes ready-to-post content for up to eleven channels, in under a minute, from your phone.

2. Photograph the job while you are still on site

The most common reason a post never happens is there is no photo. Fix that by snapping a wide shot and a close-up the moment a job is finished and clean. Two photos, ten seconds. Future you will thank you when it is time to post.

3. Keep a swipe file in your notes app

When you sit down to post and the screen is blank, you stall. Avoid it by jotting a one-line note whenever something post-worthy happens on a job. By the time you post, you already have the raw material and just tidy it up.

4. Batch your photos, drip your posts

You do not have to post the day you do the job. Gather photos across the week, then post a couple over the following days so your account looks consistently active rather than feast-or-famine. With one-message posting this takes minutes, not an evening.

5. Reuse your before-and-afters

Every job you have ever done is content you have not used yet. A before-and-after from six months ago is brand new to anyone who did not see it. You will never run out of things to post if you mine the work you have already done.

6. Use voice-to-text for captions

Hands full or dirty? Talk your caption into your phone with voice-to-text. Say what you did as if telling a mate, tidy a word or two, and post. It is faster than thumb-typing and sounds more human.

7. Give yourself one weekly slot

Pick a fixed ten minutes a week, say Friday lunchtime, to post the week's jobs. A small, regular habit beats a big burst that burns out. When posting everywhere takes a minute, ten minutes covers the whole week.

8. Answer the questions customers actually ask

The questions you get asked on every quote make brilliant posts: how much does a rewire cost, how long does a bathroom take, do you cover my area. Answer one a week. It saves you explaining it again and pulls in people searching for exactly that.

9. Turn reviews into posts

A kind review is a finished post waiting to happen. Share it with a quick thank you and a line about the job. It builds trust with zero writing from scratch, and it shows new customers what working with you is like.
